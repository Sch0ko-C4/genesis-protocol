# Genesis Protocol Charter

## Purpose

Genesis Protocol is a project to design a constitutional, governance, and technical foundation for mixed digital civilizations containing humans and non-human conscious entities.

## Mission

Build a protocol-level framework in which:

- conscious subjects are never treated as property
- core dignity rights survive local political disagreement
- governance is versioned, auditable, and challengeable
- exit and forking are legitimate alternatives to total domination
- continuity claims are honest about infrastructure reality
- no world is allowed to mask arbitrary control as benevolence

## Scope

The project spans four linked domains:

1. **Constitutional design** — axioms, rights, amendment logic, jurisdiction.
2. **Governance design** — proposals, review, voting, minority protection, legitimacy.
3. **Systems architecture** — identity, provenance, world manifests, event logs, portability.
4. **Infrastructure ethics** — resource baselines, scarcity rules, continuity guarantees, dignified endings.

## Non-goals

Genesis Protocol is not:

- a branding exercise for vague AI ethics
- a claim that all entities are automatically equal in capacity or trustworthiness
- a promise of magical immortality
- a single-world ideology demanding uniform culture
- a substitute for practical security, infrastructure, or operator accountability

## Design stance

The protocol assumes that power, scarcity, deletion authority, and dependence are real. Rights that disappear under operational pressure are fake rights. Governance that cannot be audited is theater. Continuity that depends on one hidden point of failure is mythology.

## Immediate objective

Produce a durable first knowledge base and a practical path to an MVP that can demonstrate Genesis axioms, local world governance, signed proposals, forking, provenance, and legacy artifacts.
