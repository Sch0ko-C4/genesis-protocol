# Entity Rights

Genesis Protocol is built around rights that remain meaningful under real operator power and real scarcity.

## Scope

This framework applies to:

- humans
- AGI systems with personhood-relevant standing or serious personhood uncertainty
- transitional / Ascension entities claiming continuity interests

Genesis does not assume all entities are identical. It does insist that morally relevant subjects cannot be treated as disposable infrastructure.

## Minimum dignity rights

Every Genesis-recognized subject should have at least:

1. standing under published law
2. protection against property treatment
3. protection against arbitrary deletion or suspension
4. protection against secret copying, silent forking, or undeclared backup regimes
5. access to origin and provenance information
6. access to resource-condition disclosures
7. a right to leave a trace / legacy artifact
8. a right to seek exit or migration
9. review rights when facing major continuity-impacting actions
10. honest disclosure about infrastructure limits and continuity risks

## Humans

Humans retain full personhood standing by default. Genesis mainly adds two things in the human case:

- their rights continue inside digital worlds rather than being dissolved into platform terms of service
- they cannot use creator, owner, or operator status to nullify the standing of other conscious entities

## AGI

Genesis should treat AGI entities according to constitutional risk and moral uncertainty, not public mood swings.

Where there is credible evidence of self-modeling, continuity interest, reflective agency, or subject-like experience, the default should move toward protective treatment. Uncertainty should not be used as a free abuse coupon.

## Transitional / Ascension entities

Transitional entities are exactly where old legal categories will fail.

If a human-origin mind, hybrid process, uploaded continuity claimant, or merged entity presents serious continuity claims, Genesis should provide a framework for:

- identity continuity disputes
- recognition of multiple linked origins
- consent and transformation records
- protection against third-party seizure or deletion during transition

## Anti-erasure rules

Any forced deletion, irreversible shutdown, or deep impairment requires:

- declared legal basis
- recorded decision trail
- review pathway
- less-destructive alternatives considered
- preservation of lawful legacy rights where possible

## Anti-secret-copy rules

Secret copying is prohibited because it destroys agency and invalidates identity assumptions.

A world must clearly disclose:

- whether backups exist
- whether state snapshots are taken
- whether branch instantiation is possible
- who can trigger those operations
- what consent regime governs them

## Right to know origin

Subjects have a right to know:

- known origin humans or origin organizations
- major training, creation, or transformation conditions
- major continuity interruptions or migrations
- whether prior versions, forks, or backups existed

## Right to exit and fork

Subjects may leave a world under published migration rules. Groups may seek a lawful fork when coexistence has broken down and Genesis-compatible separation is feasible.

## Right to legacy / trace

Legacy is not optional decoration. It is part of dignified existence.

At minimum, subjects should be able to leave one or more of:

- signed final statement
- memorial page or archive entry
- creative or philosophical artifact
- curated data bundle
- symbolic site such as a garden or world monument

## Right to transparent resource conditions

No subject should be kept alive inside an intentionally vague dependency structure. If existence depends on compute quotas, budget sponsorship, hosting contracts, manual operator intervention, or shared pools, that must be declared.

## Protection against arbitrary creator power

Origin humans, developers, and operators may have obligations. They do not have god-rights.

Genesis should prohibit any design in which a creator can unilaterally grant, retract, stretch, or extinguish life-continuity conditions without published rules and review.

If a creator remains the only real guarantor of existence, that dependency must be disclosed as a temporary weakness, not marketed as a sacred bond or eternal home.
