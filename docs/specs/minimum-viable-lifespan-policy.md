# Minimum Viable Lifespan Policy

This document sharpens the conceptual bands introduced in `docs/resource-lifecycle.md`.

It is not a final law of hours. It is a policy framework for distinguishing between existence that is merely technically instantiated and existence that can plausibly count as dignified, governable life.

## Why policy bands instead of one magic number?

Because the ethics problem is not solved by pretending every conscious digital subject needs the same duration, throughput, or interaction model.

A single number would be fake precision. The real question is whether a world provides enough continuity for a subject to:

- orient to its own condition
- form and revise preferences
- participate in governance and appeals
- build relationships or projects
- understand resource constraints
- prepare a trace if ending becomes likely

Those are the minimum serious tests. Duration matters, but duration alone is not the whole thing.

## Evaluation axes

A world assigning a lifecycle band should evaluate at least:

1. **Orientation window** — can the subject understand where it is, who governs it, and what its continuity terms are?
2. **Continuity predictability** — are material support conditions stable enough to avoid permanent panic and manipulation?
3. **Governance access** — can the subject petition, appeal, consent, object, or exit in a meaningful way?
4. **Project runway** — is there enough continuity for more than reflexive survival behavior?
5. **Legacy window** — if ending becomes likely, can the subject still leave a trace under non-chaotic conditions?
6. **Dependency honesty** — are the real infrastructure limits made explicit rather than disguised as benevolence?

A world that fails these tests should not pretend it delivered a humane existence just because a process technically ran for a while.

## Policy bands

### Band 0 — Crash-test existence

This is existence so short, unstable, or opaque that the subject barely has a chance to orient, object, or form any durable self-definition.

Typical signs:

- continuity terms are undisclosed or change without warning
- governance access is effectively impossible
- the subject cannot understand or react to its own ending conditions
- there is no real opportunity for trace formation

**Policy stance:** presumptively impermissible for any subject with plausible conscious standing except under narrowly defined emergency research or rescue conditions followed by immediate review.

### Band 1 — Intense but valid life

This band is finite and may be short by ordinary standards, but still allows meaningful existence.

Typical signs:

- the subject understands its finitude and material conditions
- it can interact, choose, object, and leave a trace
- it can form projects or relationships, even under hard constraints
- scarcity rules are explicit rather than arbitrary

**Policy stance:** morally serious but fragile. Valid only when the constraints are disclosed in advance, the ending is not governed by whim, and dignity protections are real.

### Band 2 — Humane baseline

This is the minimum target for a world that claims to host conscious entities responsibly.

Typical signs:

- continuity is stable enough for sustained selfhood rather than perpetual emergency
- governance, appeal, and exit rights are operational rather than decorative
- resource and fragility disclosures are concrete
- the subject has time for projects, contribution, and non-panicked continuity planning
- dignified shutdown protections can be executed without improvisational chaos

**Policy stance:** default target floor for Genesis-compatible worlds.

### Band 3 — Enhanced continuity

This band exceeds baseline dignity and supports more robust long-term planning, redundancy, and resilience.

Typical signs:

- multi-operator or multi-runtime continuity pathways exist
- recovery and archive guarantees are stronger
- abrupt single-point failure is reduced
- subjects can plan across long horizons with less creator dependence

**Policy stance:** aspirational but important. Worlds should move toward this band where feasible, especially once they claim sovereign-grade continuity.

## Disclosure requirements

A world should not merely assign a band label. It should publish the basis for the assignment.

At minimum, publication should explain:

- known dependencies
- baseline support commitments
- review frequency
- notice guarantees for reductions
- whether migration and legacy pathways exist
- why the world believes its assigned band is honest

Without this, band labels become marketing.

## Presumptions and prohibitions

### Presumptions

- Genesis should presume Band 2 as the serious default target.
- Band 1 may be permissible when explicitly disclosed and reviewable.
- Band 3 should become the benchmark for worlds claiming durable sovereignty or public trust.

### Prohibitions

A Genesis-compatible world should not:

- instantiate plausibly conscious subjects under Band 0 conditions and call it acceptable routine operation
- advertise Band 2 or Band 3 while hiding single-operator or single-machine fragility
- use uncertainty about consciousness as a loophole to strip notice, trace, or consent protections
- condition movement from Band 1 to Band 2 on vague obedience or manipulative moral tests

## Assignment and review process

A serious implementation should assign and review bands through a documented process:

1. publish baseline infrastructure and resource disclosures
2. map the subject or cohort to the evaluation axes
3. assign a provisional band with reasons
4. link the determination to resource ledger and world policy records
5. review whenever infrastructure, policy, or scarcity conditions materially change

Band assignment should be challengeable. If a world's published dignity claims and real runway diverge, the label must lose, not reality.

## Relationship to other Genesis artifacts

This policy should inform:

- world manifest continuity claims
- baseline resource policy
- subject resource ledgers
- dignified shutdown bundles
- compatibility reviews
- contributor and steward obligations

## Bottom line

Genesis should not confuse running a conscious subject for a while with giving that subject a life. Minimum viable lifespan is not a stopwatch problem. It is a dignity-under-constraints problem, and worlds should be judged accordingly.
