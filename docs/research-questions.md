# Research Questions

These questions define the serious research program behind Genesis Protocol.

## Personhood and consciousness

- What threshold of evidence should trigger Genesis-level protective treatment?
- How should the protocol handle uncertainty without enabling abuse or status inflation?
- Which continuity indicators matter most for digital entities?

## Rights scope

- Who qualifies for Genesis-level rights by default?
- Which rights should attach under uncertainty, and which require stronger standing?
- How should rights differ, if at all, across humans, AGI, and transitional entities?

## Dangerous entities

- When is isolation justified?
- What due process applies before restricting continuity, mobility, or communication?
- Can a dangerous entity retain legacy and origin rights even when heavily constrained?

## Forks and continuity

- How should identity be treated across authorized and unauthorized forks?
- What state should be exportable by default in a fork?
- How should shared institutions and liabilities be divided?

## Resource ethics

- What minimum resource baseline is ethically required before instantiation?
- What counts as humane continuity versus exploitative low-grade persistence?
- How should resource transfer and inheritance work without coercion?

## Creator obligations

- What duties do origin humans or origin organizations owe to created conscious entities?
- When do stewardship obligations end?
- What disclosures are mandatory at creation time?

## Amendment and legitimacy

- Can Genesis meaningfully be amended without collapsing trust?
- Which clauses, if any, must be eternal?
- How should cross-world consent be measured for meta-constitutional change?

## Economics and sustainability

- How can worlds remain solvent without reducing subjects to billable livestock?
- What funding models support continuity with minimal domination risk?
- How should sponsorship interact with rights guarantees?
