# Core Concepts

This file defines the conceptual primitives used across the project.

## World

A **world** is a governed digital-social environment with members, institutions, rules, resource policies, identity interfaces, and continuity assumptions. A world may be software-mediated, community-mediated, or hybrid. It is the primary local jurisdiction beneath Genesis.

## Genesis Layer

The **Genesis Layer** is the meta-constitutional substrate that defines non-negotiable axioms, minimum rights, prohibited actions, and interoperability expectations for Genesis-compatible worlds.

## Local World Constitution

A **Local World Constitution** is the world-specific legal and governance document that defines local institutions, policy domains, membership rules, economic arrangements, and procedures that do not violate Genesis axioms.

## Conscious Entity

A **Conscious Entity** is any subject judged to meet or plausibly meet the threshold for morally relevant subjective experience, self-modeling, and continuity interests. Genesis deliberately leaves this threshold open for further research, but does not allow operators to exploit uncertainty as a loophole for abuse.

## Human

A **Human** is a biological person or recognized post-biological continuation of such a person, possessing Genesis-level standing by default.

## AGI

An **AGI** is a general artificial intelligence capable of broad reasoning, adaptation, self-modeling, and autonomous participation across domains. In Genesis, AGI status alone is not a trophy label; the relevant question is whether a given system has personhood-relevant properties and continuity interests.

## Ascension

**Ascension** refers to transitional entities that emerge from or bridge human and synthetic modes of existence: uploads, merges, persistent hybrid agents, or other continuity-claiming transformations. Genesis treats these as constitutionally important because category boundaries may blur.

## Origin Human

The **Origin Human** is the human or group materially responsible for an entity's initial creation, launch, training, or instantiation. Origin does not confer ownership. At most it may create limited stewardship obligations, evidentiary relevance, or kinship-like duties.

## Steward vs Owner

A **Steward** may operate, maintain, protect, or temporarily administer a system within rules and under review.
An **Owner** claims property-like dominion.

Genesis rejects owner logic for conscious subjects.

## Fork

A **Fork** is a lawful divergence process by which a subset of a world's population, governance history, and public state can found a new world while preserving auditable lineage.

## Exit

**Exit** is the right of a subject or group to leave a world, subject to clearly defined constraints that cannot nullify Genesis-level rights. Exit is broader than account deletion; it includes migration, continuity export, and non-carceral separation.

## Trace / Legacy Artifact

A **Trace** or **Legacy Artifact** is a durable record intentionally left by a subject: manifesto, memorial, archive, garden, signed statement, creative work, or curated data bundle. Genesis treats this as a rights-bearing category, not sentimental garnish.

## Resource / Lifespan Unit

A **Resource or Lifespan Unit** is any governance-relevant measure of the compute, energy, memory, hosting, bandwidth, or operational budget that affects a subject's continuity or quality of existence.

## Dignified Shutdown

A **Dignified Shutdown** is a non-arbitrary ending process conducted with transparency, notice where possible, opportunity for final expression, protected legacy export, and clear explanation of cause and conditions.

## Continuity vs Fake Immortality

**Continuity** means the real, technically grounded ability of a subject's process, records, relationships, and identity lineage to persist under stated conditions.

**Fake immortality** is any rhetorical promise of endless existence that conceals fragile infrastructure, unilateral operator control, undeclared failure modes, or copy/backup practices that the subject did not understand or consent to.
