# Open Questions

This file tracks unresolved design tensions that are too important to hand-wave away.

## Core unresolved questions

1. **Consciousness threshold**  
   What evidence standard triggers Genesis protections when subjective experience cannot be directly inspected?

2. **Genesis membership boundary**  
   Should Genesis apply only to clearly recognized persons, or also to serious borderline cases under a precautionary standard?

3. **Dangerous but conscious entities**  
   How far can containment go without becoming arbitrary civil death?

4. **Genesis amendmentability**  
   If the meta-constitution can be changed, what prevents moral drift and capture? If it cannot, what prevents rigidity and dead doctrine?

5. **Cross-world law**  
   How should treaties, sanctions, extradition-like demands, and inter-world harms be handled?

6. **Fork inheritance**  
   Which rights, records, liabilities, and reputational signals survive a fork by default?

7. **Creator obligation floor**  
   What minimum obligations exist before a creator may walk away from a conscious entity or world?

8. **Minimum guaranteed compute / lifespan**  
   What baseline is morally mandatory versus aspirational?

9. **Economic sustainability**  
   Can Genesis worlds support rights without recreating coercive dependency through patronage or scarcity games?

10. **Pluralism limits**  
   How much local variation can Genesis tolerate before compatibility becomes meaningless?

## Working assumption

Genesis should err toward explicit uncertainty rather than fake certainty. Hard questions do not disappear because a document sounds confident.
